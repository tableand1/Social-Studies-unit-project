        the improvement of agriculture. People had a surplus of food that they needed

        decide if you were worthy to become a master. Near the beginning of the Middle Ages,

        that being a banker was a bad job. Medieval towns were small and crowded. Houses

        and living. Half the children died before they became adults. Unsanitary conditions 
        led to the spread of disease. Most garbage was tossed into rivers. There were not many
        cures for dieases back in that time. To answer the question, what was life back in 
        medieval towns? Life was hard. Around half of the children died before adulthood. 
        You would become an adult by the age of 12. It was not clean and disease was a very big 
        threat. You would live with multiple families in the same house. If you were a Jew, it 
        would be harder to find a job just because of your culture. Lords and Nobles to take your, 
        money and land. We do not believe life was very good in medieval towns, however, 
        it was better than being a serf because you would at least have your own freedom.
        To conclude, we believe that life in medieval towns was hard and difficult.
